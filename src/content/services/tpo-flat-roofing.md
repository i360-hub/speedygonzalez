## Why choose TPO for a flat roof in Arkansas?

TPO is a tough, rubber-like membrane made for flat roofs. We heat-weld the sheets into one sealed surface, so there is almost nothing for water to get through. It is white, so it reflects the Arkansas sun instead of soaking it up.

Shingles do not belong on a flat roof. Water sits there instead of running off, and shingles need slope to work.

Here is how flat roof options compare.

| Flat roof type | How long it lasts | Seams |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TPO membrane | 20–30 years | Heat-welded, sealed |
| Modified bitumen | 15–20 years | Torched or glued |
| Built-up (tar and gravel) | 15–25 years | Many layers |

## How much does a TPO flat roof cost in Hot Springs?

It depends on three things: the square footage, what sits on the roof, and whether the old roof comes off first. A small porch prices out very differently than a shop roof covered in vents. We measure your roof for free and put the number in writing.

Here is what drives the cost:

- **Square footage.** Bigger roof, more membrane and labor.
- **What is on the roof.** Vents, pipes, and HVAC units each need flashing by hand.
- **Tear-off or overlay.** If the old roof is soaked, it comes off first.

Wet insulation under an old membrane is common. We check for it before we quote.

## What does a TPO install look like?

We strip the old roof, check the decking, and lay down insulation board. Then we roll out the TPO membrane and heat-weld every seam into one sheet. Flashing goes around each vent and edge last.

Most flat roofs take two to four days. Weather matters. We do not weld membrane in the rain.

You get a walk-through when we finish, and we show you the seams.

## Why does my flat roof keep leaking?

Flat roofs leak because water sits on them. Ponding water works into any weak seam, old patch, or worn flashing. Once a membrane is at the end of its life, patching one spot just moves the leak.

Look for these warning signs.

| What you see | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Puddles two days after rain | Bad slope or clogged drain |
| Bubbles or ripples | Water trapped under membrane |
| Cracked, chalky surface | Membrane is worn out |
| Stains on your ceiling | Active leak, act now |
