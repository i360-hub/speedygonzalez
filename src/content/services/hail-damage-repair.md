## Why does hail damage hide from you?

Hail rarely punches a hole in your roof. It knocks the protective granules off your shingles and leaves dark bruises. From the ground, the roof looks the same as it did yesterday.

Underneath, the shingle is now bare and exposed to Arkansas sun. It breaks down years early.

The leak shows up long after your claim deadline has passed.

## How do I know if hail damaged my roof?

Check your gutters for piles of granules, and look at soft metal like vents, gutter caps, and your mailbox for dents. If soft metal is dented, your shingles took the same hit. You will not see the bruises from the ground.

Here is what to look for after a storm.

| Where to look | Sign of hail damage |
| --- | --- |
| Gutters and downspouts | Granules piled up like sand |
| Vents, caps, flashing | Round dents in soft metal |
| Shingles (up close) | Dark bruised spots, soft to touch |
| Deck, fence, AC unit | Dings and chipped paint |

Arkansas hail season runs March through June. Garland County sits right in the path.

## Do I really need an inspection if nothing leaks?

Yes. Hail damage is covered by most policies before it ever leaks, because the damage is already done to the shingle. Once granules are gone, the clock is running on your roof whether you can see it or not.

## Will my insurance pay for hail damage?

Most Arkansas homeowner policies cover hail damage to your roof. You pay your deductible and the insurer covers the rest of a covered claim. Proving the damage is the part that trips people up.

We photograph everything, write it up, and meet your adjuster at your house. We have handled hundreds of hail claims across Garland County.

If your roof is fine, we tell you it is fine. We do not talk anyone into a claim.

## What are my options after a hail claim?

You can replace the shingles with the same thing, step up to impact-rated shingles, or switch to metal. Impact-rated shingles cost more but handle the next storm better.
